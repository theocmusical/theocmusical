Casting Call

The O.C.: The Musical is an unauthorized parody of the hit teen drama TV series, The O.C., which aired on FOX from 2003-2007. The O.C.: The Musical follows the life of Ryan Atwood, a troubled 16-year-old bad boy who is abandoned by his parents, but is given a second chance by his attorney, Sandy Cohen. Ryan bonds quickly with Sandy’s nerdy son, Seth, and they quickly find themselves caught in a storm of musical teen drama.

Characters

Note: The specified age indicates the character’s age, not the actor’s age.

Ryan Atwood (16, baritenor): A bad boy as a result of his rough upbringing in Chino, California. Silent, strong type with sex appeal. Experience with stage combat would be an asset for this role.

Seth Cohen (16, tenor): Nerdy, Jewish loner with a keen interest in graphic novels, video games, and indie bands. Seth must be a fast talker with excellent comedic timing, and a strong singer.

Marissa Cooper (16, alto): Always seen with a flask in hand, Marissa has been dating Luke Ward since sixth grade, but is quickly enamoured by Ryan when he arrives in Newport. Marissa must be a strong comedic actor.

Summer Roberts (16, mezzo): Marissa’s materialistic, judgmental best friend with a penchant for the word “ew”. Summer’s bitchy attitude is a guard for her softer side.

Trey Atwood (20, baritenor): Ryan’s older brother, who is a multiple-time criminal offender. Trey gets Ryan involved with stealing cars and is generally unstable. Experience with stage combat would be an asset for this role.

Luke Ward (16, tenor): A vain, pretty jock who has been dating Marissa since sixth grade.

Julie Cooper (45, alto/baritone): Marissa’s overbearing mother with “MILF” sex appeal. May be played by a woman or a man in drag.

Sandy Cohen (45, baritone): Seth’s Jewish father. He is a public defender who takes Ryan, his client, in off the streets. Attempting to be a cool dad, Sandy cracks lame jokes that embarrass Seth.

Kirsten Cohen (45, mezzo): Seth’s non-Jewish mother; daughter of a rich real estate mogul. She is nervous about Ryan living in her home.

Holly Fischer (16): Typical mean girl who comes between Luke and Marissa.

J'Lynne (16): Fallen-from-grace out of Newport’s high society, J’Lynne is now poor and destitute, living in Chino.

Brice, Chadwick, Preston, Theo - Waterpolo Team (16): Macho and self-congratulatory. The waterpolo team must be strong dancers.

Crystal, Naomi, Tatiana - Harbor Girls (16): Rich, private school girls that party hard.
